# NSW Opportunity Class (OC) Placement Test Curriculum

> For Year 4 students seeking entry into Year 5 Opportunity Classes.
> Source: NSW Department of Education, NESA Stage 2 Syllabus, ACER.

---

## Test Overview

| Section | Questions | Duration | Answer Format |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Reading | 14 questions (~25 answer items) | 30-40 min | Multiple choice |
| Mathematical Reasoning | 35 questions | 40 min | Multiple choice (A-E) |
| Thinking Skills | 30 questions | 30 min | Multiple choice (A-D) |

- No calculators, rulers, or dictionaries allowed
- No penalty for wrong answers
- Paper provided for working out

---

## 1. Mathematical Reasoning

Based on the NSW Mathematics K-10 Syllabus (2022), Stage 2 (Years 3-4).
Questions go beyond regular Year 4 difficulty — they require flexible thinking.

### 1.1 Number and Algebra

#### Place Value
- Represent numbers to at least tens of thousands
- Understanding the value of each digit
- Rounding to nearest 10, 100, 1000
- *Example: "What number is six hundreds less than 74,251?"*

#### Whole Number Operations
- Mental and written strategies for +, -, ×, ÷
- Multi-step calculations
- Order of operations awareness
- Missing number problems (e.g., 67 - ▢ = 32 + 17)
- *Instant recall of times tables to 10×10 is essential*

#### Fractions
- Halves, quarters, thirds, fifths, eighths, sixths, tenths
- Comparing and ordering fractions
- Fractions on a number line
- Equivalent fractions
- Fraction of a quantity (e.g., "He spends 1/3 in the first shop")
- Adding/subtracting simple fractions

#### Decimals
- Represent and compare decimals to 2 decimal places
- Fraction-decimal conversions
- Money problems involving decimals

#### Number Patterns and Sequences
- Arithmetic sequences (constant difference)
- Geometric sequences (constant multiplier)
- Describe and continue patterns
- Find specific terms (e.g., "What is the 6th term?")
- *Example: 3, 6, 12, 24, … — find the difference between 4th and 6th terms*

#### Number Sentences
- Find missing values in equations
- Translate word problems into number sentences

### 1.2 Measurement

#### Length
- Metres, centimetres, millimetres
- Estimating and measuring
- Converting between units
- Perimeter of rectangles and composite shapes
- *Example: "A wire of 80 cm is bent into a rectangle with two 10 cm sides"*

#### Area
- Square centimetres and square metres
- Estimating, measuring, comparing areas
- Area of rectangles
- Area of composite and folded shapes

#### Volume and Capacity
- Litres, millilitres, cubic centimetres
- Reading scales on measuring containers
- *Example: "Water is poured from X into Y until Y contains 80 mL"*

#### Mass
- Kilograms and grams
- Comparing masses
- Word problems (e.g., feeding animals specific kg of food)

#### Time
- Analog and digital time (hours, minutes, seconds)
- Elapsed time problems crossing midnight/noon
- Calendar calculations (days between dates, day of the week)
- *Example: "Liu Wei got on a train at twenty to midnight, got off at 2:45 pm"*

### 1.3 Space and Geometry

#### 2D Shapes
- Properties of triangles, quadrilaterals, pentagons, hexagons
- Regular vs irregular shapes
- Lines of symmetry and reflections
- Combining and splitting shapes
- *Example: "Bruce cuts a regular pentagon with one straight line — what shapes result?"*

#### 3D Objects
- Prisms, pyramids, cubes
- Nets of 3D shapes
- Counting faces of assembled cubes
- Visualizing hidden/unseen faces
- *Example: "9 cubes glued together — how many faces can't be seen?"*

#### Position and Movement
- Grid maps with coordinates
- Compass directions (N, S, E, W, NE, NW, SE, SW)
- Following multi-step directional instructions
- Distance calculations on grids
- *Example: "Claire walks 30m N, 20m E, 70m S, 20m W — how far from start?"*

#### Angles
- Identifying right angles, acute, obtuse
- Counting right angles in digits/shapes
- Angles in turns and directions

### 1.4 Data and Probability

#### Data Interpretation
- Reading column graphs, bar graphs, dot plots
- Interpreting tables
- Evaluating claims about data
- *Example: "Mary makes 3 claims about a pet survey graph — which are correct?"*

#### Probability
- Equally likely outcomes
- Comparing likelihoods
- Probability with numbered counters/dice
- *Example: "Bag of counters 1-11 — evaluate 3 statements about likelihood"*

### 1.5 Working Mathematically

#### Multi-step Word Problems
- Money and shopping (unit pricing, change calculations)
- Rate and ratio (feeding animals, mixing)
- Systematic listing and counting
- Logical deduction through calculation
- *Example: "Pen $1.40, pencil 80c, pay with $10, change has exactly nine 50c coins"*

## 2. Thinking Skills

No prior subject knowledge required. Tests critical thinking and problem-solving.
Questions span literacy, numeracy, spatial, and logic domains.

### 2.1 Logical Analysis

#### Conditional Reasoning
- "If-then" statements
- Necessary vs sufficient conditions
- What must be true, could be true, cannot be true
- *Example: "To pass driving test you must have 20+ hours practice — which statement is true?"*

#### Evaluating Arguments
- Which statement best supports/undermines a claim
- Identifying relevant vs irrelevant evidence
- Strength of evidence
- *Example: "Dance should be part of school — which fact best supports this?"*

#### Identifying Mistakes in Reasoning
- Spotting logical fallacies
- Over-generalization
- Assuming correlation = causation
- Missing alternatives
- *Example: "Blue jacket with hood — it must be Jane's!" — what's the mistake?*

### 2.2 Evaluating Reasoning

#### Truth and Inference
- Given a set of facts, whose reasoning is correct?
- Distinguishing valid from invalid conclusions
- *Example: "Fire alarm scenario — Yuri says it must be real, Bindi says no alarm doesn't mean no fire"*

#### Set and Membership
- "All X are Y" — understanding categorical relationships
- Venn diagram-style thinking
- Finding unique/shared attributes
- *Example: "Alex, Ben, Carlos keep pets — which does Alex keep that neither other does?"*

### 2.3 Ordering and Arrangement

#### Positional Logic
- Seating arrangements from clues
- Ranking and sequencing
- Scheduling with constraints
- *Example: "Archie is right of Beau, Carter is left of Archie — what must be true?"*

#### Counting and Scheduling
- Systematic counting with constraints
- Calendar-based scheduling
- *Example: "Show runs Wed 12th to Sat 29th, evening daily + matinee Wed/Sat — how many shows?"*

### 2.4 Spatial Reasoning

#### Net Folding
- Which net folds into a valid die/cube
- Predicting 3D shapes from 2D nets
- *Example: "Which net makes a normal dice where opposite sides sum to 7?"*

#### Paper Folding and Cutting
- Predicting results of folding and cutting paper
- Symmetry through folding
- *Example: "Triangle paper folded once — which shape could NOT result?"*

#### Shape Assembly (Tangram-style)
- Identifying which set of pieces forms a given shape
- Pieces may be flipped/rotated
- *Example: "Which square uses the same five shapes as this one?"*

#### Mirror Images and Rotations
- Reflections across lines
- Rotational symmetry
- Mental rotation of shapes

### 2.5 Multi-step Arithmetic Puzzles

#### Speed/Distance/Time
- Relative speed problems
- *Example: "Express train 4× as fast — when did it depart?"*

#### String/Folding Puzzles
- Repeated halving with marks
- *Example: "String folded 3 times, marks 4 cm apart — how long is the string?"*

#### Improvement/Comparison
- Calculating changes and comparing
- *Example: "Which student improved the most between Year 9 and Year 10?"*

### 2.6 Subject Selection / Constraint Satisfaction

- Choosing from multiple lists with constraints
- Process of elimination
- *Example: "Choose 4 subjects from 4 lists — which can't be the 4th if you want English, history, science?"*

## 3. Reading Comprehension

### 3.1 Text Types
- Fiction / Literary prose
- Poetry
- Non-fiction / Factual texts (reports, instructions, biographies)
- Magazine articles

### 3.2 Question Formats
1. **Literary texts** — Multiple choice about narrative fiction
2. **Poetry** — Questions about meaning, devices, interpretation
3. **Factual texts (Cloze)** — Place sentences into correct positions in a text
4. **Short text matching** — Match descriptions to 4 short texts on same theme

### 3.3 Skills Assessed
- Literal comprehension (main ideas, details)
- Inference (drawing conclusions from implied information)
- Evaluation (author's purpose, bias)
- Text structure and organization
- Vocabulary in context
- Critical analysis (literary devices, tone)
